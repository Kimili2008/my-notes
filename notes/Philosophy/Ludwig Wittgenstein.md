Approach: Logic
- BEFORE
-Aristotole sets the foundations of logic
-Gottlon Frege develops the foundations of modern logic
- After
-Ideas in the *Tractatus* are used by philosophers of the Vienna Circle(维也纳学派), to develop Logical Positivism

-Wittgenstein rejects the ideas expressed in the *Tractatus* ,and begins to explore very different ways of viewing language.

Proof:
1.1 World is made up of facts, not objects. 
1.2 The world can be described by all the facts.
1.3 Language is made up of small propositions, which might be True or False. It can be shown that a complex proposition can be broken down to a truth function.
2.1 Propositions are the pictures of the world, the pictures of facts.
3.1 The true proposition has the same logic form as the world it describes.
4.1 The logic form itself can't be described by language. Because every correct sentence contains the logic form.
5.1 The limits of language are the limits of the world.
