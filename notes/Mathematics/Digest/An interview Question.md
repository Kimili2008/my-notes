Air resistance $F=kv^2$
Find $E_{lost}=f(x)$;  $E_{lost}=f(t)$

### E with respect to t
$$E_{lost}=E_{0}-E_{now}=mgh_{0}-mgh-\frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
$$E_{lost}=mgx-Ke$$
Solve a first-order differential equation
$$mv'=mg-kv^2$$
$$v=\frac{\sqrt{ mg }(D-1)}{\sqrt{ k }(D+1)}$$
Where
$$D=e^{2t\sqrt{ gk/m }}$$
Hence:
at first, $E_{lost}$Increases with increasing gradient, and becomes a straight line when t->$\infty$
